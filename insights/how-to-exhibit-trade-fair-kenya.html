How to Exhibit at a
Trade Fair in Kenya

A first-time exhibitor's guide, from booth selection to visitor engagement and what happens after the event closes.

By the Ishmar Halal Traders Expo Team · Updated July 2026

Exhibiting at your first trade fair can feel like a big step, especially for an SME weighing the cost of a booth against an uncertain return. Having organized 48+ events and worked directly with hundreds of first-time exhibitors, we have seen a consistent pattern: the businesses that prepare well before the event day almost always outperform those that show up and hope for the best.

This guide walks through what to expect and how to prepare, whether you are considering a Halal expo, a general trade fair, or a corporate exhibition.

1. Choose the Right Booth Size and Position

Booth size should match your product range and budget, not your ambition. A first-time exhibitor with a handful of products often does better with a compact, well-organized 6m² booth than a sprawling space they cannot fill with energy or stock. As your business grows and you understand what a show delivers for you, scaling up to a larger or premium corner position makes more sense.

Position matters too. Booths near entrances, food courts, or main stages tend to see more footfall. Organizers can usually advise which zones suit your product category best.

2. Prepare Your Materials Well in Advance

Signage should be clear from a distance, your business name, what you sell, and why it matters, readable in a few seconds as visitors walk past. Beyond signage, prepare a simple price list, business cards or a QR code linking to your contact details, and product samples if your category allows it. Businesses that leave this preparation to the last week often end up with rushed, inconsistent branding that undersells their product.

3. Train Your Team to Engage, Not Just Sell

The exhibitors who leave a lasting impression are rarely the loudest sellers. They are the ones who ask visitors what they are looking for, listen, and respond with a relevant answer. If you are bringing staff to represent your booth, brief them on your key messages, pricing, and how to capture a visitor's contact details naturally in conversation.

4. Capture Contact Information Systematically

A trade fair generates conversations, but conversations without a record are opportunities that quietly disappear. Have a simple system, a sign-up sheet, a QR code to a contact form, or a notebook, and use it consistently throughout the event, not just when things are quiet.

5. Follow Up Within a Week

The value of an exhibition is rarely realized on the show floor. It is realized in the follow-up. Reach out to the contacts you gathered within a week while the conversation is still fresh in their mind. A short message referencing what you discussed at your booth performs far better than a generic broadcast.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much space does a first-time exhibitor need?
It depends on your product range and how you plan to display it. Sponsorship packages at Ishmar events start from a 6m² booth for Silver sponsors up to 36m² premium corner booths for Platinum sponsors. Most first-time SMEs do well starting smaller and scaling up at future events.
What should I bring to my first trade fair as an exhibitor?
At minimum: clear signage with your business name and offering, printed or digital price lists, business cards or a QR code to your contact details, product samples if applicable, and a simple way to capture visitor contact information for follow-up.
Do I need Halal certification before exhibiting at a Halal expo?
Not necessarily. Many exhibitors join Halal expos while still working toward certification, using the event to build visibility and connect with certification specialists. Read our guide on Halal certification in Kenya to understand the process.
