Women in Business:
Navigating Kenya's Halal
Entrepreneurship Ecosystem

How women-led businesses are shaping Kenya's Halal economy, and where to find support, visibility, and networks.

By the Ishmar Halal Traders Expo Team · Updated July 2026

Women Are Already Leading Kenya's Halal Economy

Anyone who has walked the floor of a Halal trade exhibition in Kenya will notice it quickly: women-led businesses are not a minority presence. They are a defining feature of the market. Across Ishmar's events, 60% of exhibitors are women-owned businesses, spanning food production, cosmetics, fashion, and services. This is not incidental. It reflects a deliberate choice by our organization to build an inclusive platform, and it reflects the reality of who is building businesses in Kenya's Halal economy today.

For women entrepreneurs navigating this space, the opportunity is real, but so are the practical questions: where to find capital, mentorship, market access, and visibility. This article looks at what the ecosystem currently offers and how to make the most of it.

The Common Challenges

Women-led SMEs in Kenya, as elsewhere, often face a combination of limited access to formal financing, fewer established business networks, and less visibility in male-dominated trade spaces. In the Halal business context specifically, navigating certification processes and connecting with the right buyers can also feel less accessible without an existing network to draw on.

None of these challenges are unique to any one entrepreneur, which is exactly why structured platforms, mentorship, and shared spaces matter so much in closing the gap.

Where the Support Exists

Dedicated Empowerment Programs

Our Youth & Women Empowerment service includes women-led business platforms, mentorship and skills training, and thought leadership panels specifically built around the challenges women entrepreneurs face. These sessions are designed to be practical, covering everything from pricing strategy to how to prepare for an exhibition.

Exhibition Visibility

Exhibitions remain one of the fastest ways for a women-led SME to gain visibility with buyers and the public at once. Our guide on how to exhibit at a trade fair in Kenya covers the practical steps to prepare, whether it is your first booth or your fifth.

Peer Networks

One of the most underrated benefits of exhibitions and empowerment forums is simply meeting other women running similar businesses. Many of the most durable partnerships and referral relationships among our exhibitors began as a conversation between neighboring booths.

What This Looks Like in Practice

Since 2023, we have trained over 1,000 youth and entrepreneurs through our empowerment programs, with a strong emphasis on women-led business participation. These are not one-off talks. They are recurring forums built into our event calendar, alongside the mentorship and networking opportunities that come with them.

If you are a woman entrepreneur considering your next step, whether that is your first exhibition, your first mentorship session, or exploring Halal certification for your product, reaching out to our team is a good place to start. We are also actively building institutional partnerships to make participation more affordable for SMEs who need it most.

Frequently Asked Questions

What support does Ishmar offer women entrepreneurs?
Ishmar runs dedicated women-led business platforms, mentorship and skills training, and thought leadership panels as part of our Youth & Women Empowerment programs, alongside exhibition space at our events.
What percentage of exhibitors at Ishmar events are women-owned?
60% of exhibitors across Ishmar's events are women-owned businesses, reflecting our deliberate focus on inclusive participation in the Halal economy.
Do I need an established business to join a women's empowerment program?
No. Our empowerment forums and mentorship sessions are designed for entrepreneurs at different stages, including those just starting out, alongside more established women-led SMEs.
How do I get involved as a mentor or speaker for women's programs?
Contact our team through the website or WhatsApp. We welcome experienced businesswomen and professionals interested in mentoring or speaking at our empowerment forums and panels.
